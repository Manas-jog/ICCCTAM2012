Conference Overview

There has been a rapid growth in adopting the cloud computing strategy; however it is still in its infancy in providing its full potential to the users. This is because, while the technology that enables cloud computing seems fairly mature, there are challenges related to security, manageability, administration, pricing/billing for the services offered by the cloud. International Conference on Cloud Computing Technologies, Applications and Management 2012 (ICCCTAM-12) will be a platform for academicians, researchers, practitioners and users to discuss challenges in Cloud Computing and share their knowledge in the following four perspectives, but not limited to, thrust areas:

Venue

Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani (BITS Pilani), is an internationally reputed and number one private deemed university in India for higher education. It established BITS Pilani, Dubai Campus in the year 2000, to provide opportunities for quality higher education to youth in UAE and neighboring countries.


Dubai Campus, spread in approximately 14.5 acre land at Dubai International Academic City (DIAC), has international infrastructure, laboratories, library, workshop, sports facilities, and hostels. Dubai Campus offers eight Integrated First Degree Programmes, five Higher Degree Programmes and PhD in engineering and allied disciplines. The campus has more than 1600 students from over 20 countries. Over 1700 students have graduated and are employed in more than 500 prestigious companies around the world, or have completed or are pursuing graduate programs in more than 65 leading universities – a list that includes but is not limited to Cornell, Stanford, Carnegie Mellon, Wharton and IIM. Many graduates are also successful entrepreneurs.


BITS Pilani, Dubai Campus also fosters research and academics by partnering with universities – this list includes Uniformed Services University of the Health Sciences, University of Buffalo, University of Maryland, Kansas State, Purdue, Iowa State, George Mason, Ontario, and Windsor. Dubai Campus is among very few institutes in the Middle East to have active and recognized student chapters of professional bodies like IEEE, ASHRAE, ASME, SAE and ACM. Dubai Campus students were the only participants from the UAE in International Aerial Robotics Competition 2011 (IARC 2011) where on their very first appearance, they secured the first place.
